vidlingo — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-21

vidlingo (the browser extension, published by Fluxora) turns any YouTube video with captions into bilingual sentence flashcards, shown in a side panel next to the video. This page explains what data the extension touches, what it stores, and — importantly — what it never does.

The short version

Your saved decks/flashcards stay on your own device, in this browser's local storage. vidlingo does not run its own server that receives your videos, captions, or translations, and does not sell or share your data with anyone. The only data that ever leaves your device is what you explicitly send to a translation provider you configure yourself (see below).

What the extension reads

Data What happens to it
Caption/transcript text from the YouTube video open in your active tab Read locally to build flashcards. Sent off your device only if you've configured your own LLM translation provider (see below) — a request goes directly from your browser to that provider, never through vidlingo's own servers.
Your saved library (decks, cards, bookmarks) Stored only in this browser's local storage (chrome.storage.local). Never transmitted anywhere.
Downloaded dictionary/tokenizer data (per learning language) Fetched, after your confirmation, from a public GitHub repository hosting static files — no personal data is sent to request these, and nothing downloads without you confirming it first. Stored locally (IndexedDB).

Translation providers (bring your own key)

Translation is provided by whichever option you choose in Settings:

  • Chrome's built-in on-device translator — nothing ever leaves your device.
  • Any OpenAI-compatible LLM API endpoint (OpenAI, Groq, OpenRouter, a local LM Studio/Ollama server, etc.) — your API key is stored only in this browser's local storage, and requests go directly from your browser to that endpoint.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell or share your data with third parties.
  • We don't track your browsing history beyond the single active tab's current YouTube URL, read only to know which video is open.
  • We don't show ads or use your data for advertising.
  • We don't use your data to determine creditworthiness or for lending purposes.

Permissions this extension requests

PermissionWhy
storageSaves your library and downloaded dictionaries locally on this device.
sidePanelThe entire UI is a side panel next to the video, not a popup.
cookiesRequired by a sign-in library the extension bundles but does not currently expose any sign-in flow for — unused in this build.
Host access to youtube.comReads the transcript already visible on the page you have open, and adds playback controls to it.
Host access to github.com / githubusercontent.comDownloads a language's dictionary/tokenizer data, only after you confirm — these are public, read-only reference files, not personal data.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change, and material changes will be reflected in the extension's own Chrome Web Store listing.
