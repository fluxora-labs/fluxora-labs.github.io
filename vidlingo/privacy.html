vidlingo — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-21

vidlingo (the browser extension, published by Fluxora) turns any YouTube video with captions into bilingual sentence flashcards, shown in a side panel next to the video. This page explains what data the extension touches, what it stores, and — importantly — what it never does.

The short version

Your saved decks/flashcards stay on your own device, in this browser's local storage. vidlingo does not run its own server that receives your videos, captions, or translations, and does not sell or share your data with anyone. The only data that ever leaves your device is what you explicitly send to a translation provider you configure yourself (see below) — or, if you opt into the separate, paid Managed subscription tier, what's needed to authenticate and meter that subscription.

What the extension reads

Data What happens to it
Caption/transcript text from the YouTube video open in your active tab Read locally to build flashcards. Sent off your device only if you've configured your own translation provider (see below) — a request goes directly from your browser to that provider, never through vidlingo's own servers, unless you've opted into the Managed tier.
Your saved library (decks, cards, bookmarks) Stored only in this browser's local storage (chrome.storage.local). Never transmitted anywhere.
Downloaded dictionary/tokenizer data (per learning language) Fetched, after your confirmation, from a public GitHub repository hosting static files — no personal data is sent to request these, and nothing downloads without you confirming it first. Stored locally (IndexedDB).

Translation providers (bring your own key)

Translation is provided by whichever option you choose in Settings:

  • Chrome's built-in on-device translator — nothing ever leaves your device.
  • DeepL, LibreTranslate, or an LLM API endpoint — your API key is stored only in this browser's local storage, and requests go directly from your browser to that provider.
  • Managed (optional, paid subscription) — for people who don't want to manage their own key. If you opt in, caption text you translate is sent to Fluxora's backend, which forwards it to an upstream translation provider on your behalf, and an authentication token (via Clerk) plus subscription/usage state is stored to verify your subscription. This tier is off by default and requires an explicit sign-up.

What we don't do

  • We don't sell or share your data with third parties.
  • We don't track your browsing history beyond the single active tab's current YouTube URL, read only to know which video is open.
  • We don't show ads or use your data for advertising.
  • We don't use your data to determine creditworthiness or for lending purposes.
